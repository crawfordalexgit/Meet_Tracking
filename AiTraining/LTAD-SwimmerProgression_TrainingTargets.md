2. Foundations of Long Term Athlete Development
2.1 Swim England LTAD Stages
Swim England defines development through progressive stages:
Stage	Typical Age	Focus
FUNdamentals	5–8	Basic movement skills, fun, water confidence
Learn to Train	8–11	Technique, aerobic foundation, skill acquisition
Train to Train	11–14	Aerobic development, stroke efficiency, discipline
Train to Compete	14–17	Event specific training, race skills, volume increase
Train to Win	17+	High performance training, double sessions, peak performance

3. Vorontsov’s Multi Year Long Term Training Principles
Andrei Vorontsov’s research emphasises:
3.1 Multi Year Development Cycles
•	Training must be planned over 8–12 years, not seasons.
•	Early years emphasise technique and aerobic base.
•	Later years emphasise event specific training and anaerobic development.
3.2 Progressive Training Load
Vorontsov identifies three pillars:
1.	Volume progression (gradual increase in weekly hours)
2.	Intensity progression (more race pace and anaerobic work)
3.	Technical refinement (continuous stroke efficiency improvement)
3.3 Biological vs. Chronological Age
•	Training load should match biological maturity, not just age.
•	Peak height velocity (PHV) is a key marker for adjusting training.
Reference: Vorontsov, A. (Various publications on multi year training, available via ResearchGate and academic journals)
